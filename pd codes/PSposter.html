    • Event Poster





    Sketch



    For my poster design, I tried to make the band stands out. I made use of rhythm, colour contrast, scale and proportion as well as asymmetrical balance as my principle of design.

    For rhythm, I assemble the instruments such that it produces a feel of movement which allows the poster to look alive and not flat. In addition, it is to also make the poster look livelier and more vibrant.

    For colour contrast, if I just cluster the band in the middle, it makes it look confusing and nothing stands out. However, when I add colour contrast to it by adding a black oval shape shadow behind them, it elevates the colour and make the focus towards the band which makes them stands out even more. In addition, for the text, I decided to make them white. This can grab the viewersâ€™ attention as it has a colour contrast.

    For asymmetrical balance, I decided to place the objects at different areas. It enhances the whole poster to make it more appealing rather than just a simple symmetrical balance throughout the poster.

    For scale and proportion, I adjust my instruments size from big to small. This allows the band to shine rather than the instruments.

    Lastly, I made the 'Free Entry' and the event name orange in colour to immediately grab the viewer's attention.
